# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Donors and corporate sponsors (trust, credibility, a clear giving path), volunteers and coaches (how to join), and parents in Lagos (free programmes, enrolment). Mixed audience; site must route each quickly. Visitors arrive on phones as often as desktops.

## Product Purpose

Static marketing/impact website for the Sports Bridge International Foundation — the social-impact arm of Sports Bridge International (Adidas-franchise retailer at Ikoyi Club 1938, Lagos). Lives at https://sportsbridgefoundation.com. Communicates the play-to-purpose pathway (Connect → Play → Grow → Rise), drives donations (Paystack) and enquiries (Formspree).

## Brand Personality

Athletic, editorial, human. A sports journal printed on a pitch: light paper ground, enormous condensed headlines, hard grid, field-marking graphics, league-table stats. Sibling of the dark/commercial SBI store — same teal, pink and condensed voice, but light, warm and human.

## Anti-references

The "AI nonprofit" look this rebuild replaces: Fraunces/serif display on cream, pill navbars, mesh gradients, emoji icons, rounded-2xl card grids with soft shadows, centered hero + two pill buttons, smiling-stock-kids montages. Also: dark theme (store's territory), gradients between brand colors.

## Design Principles

1. Field geometry carries the identity — touchlines, halfway lines, center circles, arcs as the one memorable system.
2. Stats are league tables, not floating cards. Sections are numbered like fixtures.
3. Paper dominates, ink works, teal leads, pink is scarce (≤3% of any viewport).
4. Geometry + type must stand without photography; the ~5 images used get identical teal duotone.
5. One orchestrated hero moment per page; everything else restrained and decisive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast on all text, semantic landmarks, single h1 per page, visible focus (ink 2px outline, offset), alt text everywhere, prefers-reduced-motion disables all non-essential animation, content readable and nav usable with JS disabled.
