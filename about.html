Our story

We believe talent is everywhere — opportunity isn't. We're here to change that.

The Sports Bridge International Foundation was born from a simple observation on the pitches of Lagos: the next great athlete, coach or leader is often just one opportunity away — and that opportunity rarely arrives on its own.

01

Mission & vision

Why we exist

Mission

To unlock the potential of young people across Africa by using sport as a bridge — connecting play to development, development to opportunity, and individuals to community.

Vision

An Africa where every young person, regardless of background, gender or postcode, has a clear pathway from their first game to their fullest potential — on the field and beyond it.

“We don't just hand out footballs. We build bridges that a child can keep crossing for the rest of their life.”

03

Our journey so far

From one estate pitch to a movement

2023

The first whistle

Founded as the social impact arm of Sports Bridge International, anchored within Ikoyi Club 1938 with a single weekend academy serving 60 children.

2024

Bridging digital to physical

Launched the Bridge Gaming League, reaching 2,000 young people in year one.

2025

Opening doors for girls

Girls on the Ball launched across six communities; first 50 academic scholarships awarded.

2026

Building for scale

Now reaching over 2,000 young people across 48 programmes and 16 partner schools.

What's
next

Where we're headed

Following our Lagos debut, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Kano and Ibadan have been identified as priority cities for our next phase — each hub tailored to local cultural and economic conditions and developed with state governments, sports associations, educational institutions and private-sector partners.
